Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich layers of flavor and comforting texture. It typically consists of wide sheets of pasta layered with a hearty meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and a generous topping of melted cheese such as mozzarella or Parmesan. The sauce is often made with minced beef, tomatoes, garlic, onions, and herbs like basil and oregano, creating a deep, savory taste that pairs perfectly with the soft pasta layers. Once assembled, the dish is baked until bubbling and golden on top.

This recipe is perfect for family meals or special occasions, as it can be prepared in advance and easily shared. Each bite delivers a balance of tender pasta, flavorful sauce, and creamy cheese, making it a satisfying and filling option. Serve your lasagna hot with a side of fresh salad or garlic bread for a complete and delicious meal.

  • Lasagna sheets (pasta)
  • Minced beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Chopped tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Cook onions and garlic, then add minced beef and brown it.
  2. Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, and seasoning; simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  3. (Optional) Make a white sauce with butter, flour, and milk.
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  5. Layer meat sauce, lasagna sheets, and cheese (repeat layers).
  6. Top with cheese and bake for 30–40 minutes.
  7. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
